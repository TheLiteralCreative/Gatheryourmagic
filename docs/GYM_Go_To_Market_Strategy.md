# GatherYourMagic.com
## Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

**Document Type:** Business Strategy
**Audience:** Project Owner, Marketing Team, Investors
**Date:** May 25, 2026

---

## 1. The Core Premise

The MTG market is highly fragmented but deeply networked. Players do not discover new tools through traditional advertising; they discover them through content creators, Reddit, and their Local Game Store (LGS).

The Go-To-Market strategy for GatherYourMagic relies on a "B2B2C" (Business to Business to Consumer) wedge: **We acquire the Local Game Stores first, and they acquire the players for us.**

---

## 2. Phase 1: The LGS Wedge (Months 1-3)

The commissioning comic shop is Patient Zero. We use them to perfect the Store Tier, and then we use their success to acquire other stores.

### 2.1 The "Free Kiosk" Offer
- **The Pitch:** We approach 50 regional LGS owners with a specific offer: "We will digitize your MTG inventory and give you a customer-facing search kiosk for free for 6 months, in exchange for your feedback."
- **The Value to the Store:** They stop losing sales because employees can't find cards. They look professional.
- **The Value to GatherYourMagic:** We get massive inventory data, real-world stress testing, and captive audiences in 50 stores.

### 2.2 The Viral Loop (The Player Acquisition)
When a customer uses the kiosk in the store, the kiosk prompts them:
*"Want to see if we have cards for your deck before you drive here? Create a free GatherYourMagic account."*
The store acts as our physical acquisition channel. Every store brings in ~100-300 local players.

---

## 3. Phase 2: Content Creator Partnerships (Months 4-6)

Once the platform has a stable base of local players and stores, we move to the digital ecosystem.

### 3.1 The "Sponsored Deck" Strategy
Instead of paying for standard pre-roll ads on YouTube, we pay MTG content creators (e.g., The Command Zone, Tolarian Community College) to **use the platform in their content**.
- **The Execution:** The creator builds their deck for the episode using GatherYourMagic. They use the AI Coach on-screen to make a final cut. The link in the description goes to their GatherYourMagic deck page.
- **The Hook:** "Want to play my deck? Click the link to view it on GatherYourMagic, and see which of your local stores has the missing pieces."

### 3.2 The Affiliate Program
We launch an affiliate program where creators get 30% of the first year's subscription revenue for any user who signs up for Premium/Pro through their link. This incentivizes them to mention the platform organically.

---

## 4. Phase 3: Community Seeding (Months 7-12)

MTG players gather on Reddit (`r/magicTCG`, `r/EDH`) and Discord. Direct promotion is banned or ignored. We must provide utility.

### 4.1 "The Oracle" Discord Bot
We package the AI Oracle into a free Discord bot. Server admins can install it, allowing players to tag `@GatherOracle` to settle rules disputes mid-game. Every response from the bot includes a subtle link: *"Analyzed by GatherYourMagic.com."*

### 4.2 The "Meta Report"
We use the aggregated data from the Playstyle Profiler and win/loss tracking to publish high-quality, data-driven articles on Reddit. E.g., *"We analyzed 10,000 Commander decks. Here's why Timmy players are actually winning more than Spike players this month."* This establishes the platform as an authority.

---

## 5. Pricing & Monetization Ramp

- **Launch:** The platform launches entirely free for 30 days to build the user base and train the AI models.
- **Day 31:** The Feature Gates activate. The Free tier remains robust, but power users hit the paywall for AI features.
- **Store Tier Billing:** Begins after the 6-month beta period for the initial 50 stores.

---

## 6. Summary of Acquisition Channels

| Channel | Target Audience | Expected Cost of Acquisition (CAC) | Scalability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Local Game Stores (LGS) | Casual & Competitive Players | Low (Time/Sales effort) | Medium |
| Content Creators | Broad MTG Community | High (Sponsorship fees) | High |
| Discord Bot | Enfranchised Playgroups | Very Low (Server costs) | Very High |
| Data Journalism (Reddit) | Competitive Grinders | Low (Content creation time) | Medium |

By treating the Local Game Store not just as a customer, but as an acquisition channel, GatherYourMagic bypasses the expensive digital ad market and goes straight to where the players actually buy their cards.
