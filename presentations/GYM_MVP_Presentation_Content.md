### Slide 2: The Problem We're Solving
**Heading:** A Great Collection Deserves a Great System

**Body:**
Your store has a significant Magic: The Gathering inventory — but right now, finding a specific card means digging through binders, boxes, and memory. Customers ask if you have a card. Employees aren't sure. Sales are lost.

The problem isn't the collection. The problem is the lack of a system.

**Key points:**
- Cards are difficult to locate quickly during a customer interaction
- No reliable way for customers to check availability before visiting the store
- Inventory tracking is inconsistent, making it hard to know what you actually have
- Missed sales = lost revenue from an asset you already own

---

### Slide 3: What We're Building
**Heading:** GatherYourMagic Turns Your Inventory Into a Searchable Database

**Body:**
GatherYourMagic is a purpose-built platform for exactly this problem. The MVP delivers three core capabilities, designed specifically around how a local game store operates.

**Three deliverables:**
1. **The Inventory System** — A complete, searchable digital catalogue of your entire MTG collection, with condition, location, and pricing for every card.
2. **The In-Store Kiosk** — A locked-down search terminal customers can use on a tablet or screen in your store to find cards themselves.
3. **The Public Storefront** — A public web page at gatheryourmagic.com/stores/[yourstore] where customers can browse your inventory from home before they visit.

---

### Slide 4: The Inventory System
**Heading:** Every Card, Catalogued and Findable in Seconds

**Body:**
The inventory system is the foundation. Every card in your collection is entered once and stored in a structured database that can be searched instantly by any employee or customer.

**What gets tracked for each card:**
- Card name, set, and edition
- Condition (Mint, Near Mint, Lightly Played, Moderately Played, Heavily Played, Damaged)
- Foil or non-foil status
- Your asking price
- Physical location in the store (e.g., "Showcase A", "Binder 3", "Backroom Box 12")
- Current market value (updated nightly from market data)

**Getting the data in:** Cards can be added one at a time through the search interface, or imported in bulk via a spreadsheet (CSV) upload — ideal for getting your existing inventory digitized quickly.

---

### Slide 5: The In-Store Kiosk
**Heading:** Customers Find What They Need — Without Asking Every Time

**Body:**
The Kiosk Mode transforms any tablet, laptop, or monitor in your store into a customer-facing search terminal. It is locked to a read-only view of your inventory — customers can search and browse, but they cannot change anything.

**What a customer sees on the kiosk:**
- Search bar with instant autocomplete (results appear as they type)
- Card image, condition, and your price
- Physical location in the store ("Ask an employee for Binder 3")
- How many copies you have in stock

**What this means for your staff:**
Instead of interrupting an employee to ask "do you have a Rhystic Study?", the customer checks the kiosk, finds it in Binder 4, and brings it to the register. Your staff spends less time searching and more time selling.

---

### Slide 6: The Public Storefront
**Heading:** Your Inventory Is Open 24/7 — Even When the Store Is Closed

**Body:**
Every store on GatherYourMagic gets a public URL — a live web page that shows your current inventory to anyone with a browser. Local players can check what you have before making the trip.

**Example:** `gatheryourmagic.com/stores/mycomicshop`

**What this does for your business:**
- Customers plan visits around what you have in stock, increasing conversion when they arrive
- Players searching for a specific card on GatherYourMagic see a notification: "Available at a store near you" — driving foot traffic directly to you
- Your store appears in local search results for Magic: The Gathering cards
- No extra work required — the storefront updates automatically as your inventory changes

### Slide 7: The MVP Timeline
**Heading:** From Commission to Live System in 8 Weeks

**Body:**
The MVP is scoped to deliver exactly what was commissioned — no more, no less. The timeline is structured so that the most critical piece (the inventory system) is completed first, with the customer-facing features built on top of it.

**Phase breakdown:**

| Week | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Week 1 | Infrastructure setup, hosting, and domain configuration |
| Weeks 2–3 | Database foundation — all 30,000+ MTG cards loaded and searchable |
| Weeks 4–5 | User accounts, store account setup, and secure login |
| Week 6 | Card search interface — fast, fuzzy, autocomplete |
| Weeks 7–8 | Store inventory management, Kiosk Mode, and Public Storefront |
| End of Week 8 | **Beta launch with your store as the first live client** |

### Slide 8: What Happens After the MVP
**Heading:** The MVP Is the Foundation — Not the Ceiling

**Body:**
The platform is being built on an architecture designed to grow. Once the comic shop MVP is live and validated, the full GatherYourMagic platform expands to serve individual collectors, competitive players, and additional game stores — with no rebuild required.

**What comes next (already planned):**
- Personal collection management for individual players
- AI-powered deck building coach and rules referee
- Playstyle profiling for personalized recommendations
- Premium subscription tiers for power users
- Support for Pokémon, Yu-Gi-Oh!, and other card games

Your store will be the proof of concept that demonstrates what the platform can do — and the first entry in a growing network of local game stores that drives players to your door.

### Slide 9: What We Need to Get Started
**Heading:** Three Things to Kick Off the Build

**Body:**
The project is ready to begin. To move from planning to development, three things are needed from the client side:

1. **A card inventory export or list** — In any format (spreadsheet, handwritten list, photos of binders). This becomes the first data import and the first test of the system.
2. **Store details** — Name, address, and preferred public URL slug (e.g., `/stores/mycomicshop`).
3. **A point of contact** — One person on the store's team who will be the primary tester and feedback provider during the 8-week build.

Everything else is handled on the development side.
